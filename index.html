Script Training

Master effective sales techniques and client communication for insurance appointments

Opening Script

Introduction & Rapport Building

"Hi [Client Name], thank you for taking the time to meet with me today. I'm [Your Name] from [Agency Name], and I appreciate you allowing me to review your insurance needs."

"Before we dive into the details, I'd love to learn a bit more about you. How long have you been in your current position? What do you enjoy most about your work?"

"That's fantastic! Now, I want to make sure you understand exactly why we're here today. We're not just talking about insurance - we're talking about protecting everything you've worked so hard to build. Does that make sense?"

The Liability Lesson

Why Liability Limits Matter

"Let me ask you a question that might seem a bit dramatic, but it's crucial to understand: What would happen if you were in an accident and were found legally responsible for seriously injuring someone?"

"I want you to think about this scenario: You accidentally rear-end a doctor in a Lexus at a stoplight. That doctor has to go to the emergency room, needs surgery, and can't work for 6 months. This doctor makes $500,000 per year."

"Let's break down the potential costs:"

  • Emergency room and surgery: $150,000
  • 6 months of lost income (doctor): $250,000
  • Ongoing medical care: $100,000
  • Pain and suffering: $200,000
  • Total potential liability: $700,000

"Now, here's the critical question: Do you have $700,000 sitting in your checking account to cover this if your insurance doesn't? Most people don't - and that's exactly why we have liability insurance."

Asset Protection Logic

Connecting Assets to Coverage Needs

"Let me show you how we determine what you need. We look at five key areas of your financial picture:"

1. Your Home ($[home value])

This represents your family's shelter and biggest asset. If someone sues you for more than your liability coverage, they can attach your home.

2. Your Vehicle ($[vehicle value])

Not just the car's value, but the potential damage you could cause in an accident.

3. Your Income (${[income]}/year)

We typically calculate 10 times your annual income as the minimum liability protection you should have.

4. Life Insurance Gap ($[life insurance difference])

If you don't have enough life insurance, liability claims could affect your family's future.

5. Investment Accounts ($[investments])

Your retirement and investment accounts need protection from liability claims.

"Based on your specific situation, your total liability exposure is $[calculated exposure]. This means you should have at least that much in liability coverage - probably more."

Insurance Tier Recommendations

Explaining Coverage Levels

"Let me explain the different coverage tiers and why we recommend certain levels:"

Tier 1: Up to $100,000

Who it's for: Basic coverage for minimal assets

Reality check: This barely covers a minor accident. In today's world, this is like wearing a raincoat in a hurricane.

Tier 2: $100,000 - $300,000

Who it's for: Moderate asset protection

Reality check: Better, but still insufficient for most middle-class families. One serious accident could wipe out your savings.

Tier 3: $300,000 - $500,000

Who it's for: Solid protection for established families

Reality check: This is where most families should be. It provides real protection without being excessive.

Tier 4: $500,000+

Who it's for: High net worth individuals and those wanting maximum protection

Reality check: For most people, this is the smart choice. The peace of mind is worth the extra cost.

"Based on your assets and risk profile, we recommend [specific tier]. This gives you the best balance of protection and value."

Closing Techniques

Effective Closing Strategies

1. The Assumption Close

"Great! Based on everything we've discussed, I have the perfect policy for you. The [tier] coverage we're recommending will give you exactly what you need. How does Tuesday work for getting your policy started?"

2. The Summary Close

"Let me make sure I have this right: You have $[assets] in total assets, your liability exposure is $[exposure], and we agree that you need at least $[recommended] in coverage to properly protect your family. Sound correct?"

"Perfect. Then the [tier] plan is exactly what you need. Shall we get the paperwork started?"

3. The Choice Close

"I have two options for you today. We can start with the [tier] plan, which I recommend, or we could do the [lower tier] plan. Based on everything we've discussed, which one feels right to you?"

4. The Urgency Close

"I want to be upfront with you - in my experience, people who delay making this decision often end up wishing they had acted sooner. You're here because you understand the importance of protecting your family. What questions do you have about getting started today?"

Follow-Up Scripts

Post-Appointment Communication

Same Day Follow-Up

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Agency]. I wanted to thank you for taking time to meet with me today. I know we covered a lot of information, and I wanted to make sure you have my contact information if any questions come up. I'll be sending over the formal quotes by end of business tomorrow. Does that work for you?"

Quote Delivery

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I have your insurance quotes ready. I wanted to go over them with you personally rather than just sending an email. Do you have 10 minutes this afternoon to review them together? I think you're going to be pleased with what we found."

Closing Follow-Up

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I wanted to check in and see if you've had a chance to review the information I sent over. I know it's an important decision, and I want to make sure you have everything you need to move forward. What questions can I answer for you?"

Pro Tips

Advanced Sales Techniques

  • Listen More Than You Talk: Let clients tell you about their concerns and goals.
  • Use Their Name: People love hearing their own name. Use it naturally throughout the conversation.
  • Paint Pictures: Use vivid examples and scenarios to help clients visualize the protection they need.
  • Address Objections Head-On: Don't avoid difficult topics - address them directly with facts and empathy.
  • Focus on Benefits, Not Features: Always connect policy features back to what they mean for the client and their family.
  • Create Urgency Ethically: Emphasize the importance of protection without creating fear or pressure.
  • Follow Up Consistently: Most sales happen after the first meeting. Persistence pays off.
